Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services, appointments, insurance, and more.

How do I schedule an appointment?

You can schedule an appointment in several ways:

  • Through our patient portal
  • Calling our office at (256) -740-470-116
  • Visiting our clinic in person
  • Using our mobile app

For new patients, we recommend calling our office directly to set up your first appointment and complete necessary paperwork.

What should I bring to my appointment?

Please bring the following items to your appointment:

  • Photo ID (driver's license, passport, etc.)
  • Insurance card
  • List of current medications
  • Medical records or previous test results (if applicable)
  • Payment method for co-pays or deductibles
  • Completed new patient forms (if you're a new patient)
How do I cancel or reschedule my appointment?

To cancel or reschedule your appointment, please notify us at least 24 hours in advance. You can do this by:

  • Logging into your patient portal
  • Calling our office at (256) 773-403-968
  • Using our mobile app

Please note that late cancellations or no-shows may result in a fee.

What insurance plans do you accept?

We accept most major insurance plans, including:

  • Blue Cross Blue Shield
  • Aetna
  • Cigna
  • United Healthcare
  • Medicare
  • Medicaid

Please contact our billing department at (256) 702-465-907 ext. 2 to verify if we accept your specific insurance plan.

How do I pay my bill?

You can pay your bill in several ways:

  • Online through our patient portal
  • By phone at (256) 702-665-638 ext. 2
  • By mail to our billing address
  • In person at our clinic

We accept credit cards, debit cards, checks, and cash payments.

What services do you offer?

We offer a comprehensive range of healthcare services, including:

  • Primary care
  • Specialized care (cardiology, orthopedics, neurology, etc.)
  • Preventive care and wellness services
  • Diagnostic services (lab tests, imaging)
  • Telemedicine consultations
  • Minor surgical procedures
  • Physical therapy

Visit our Services page for more detailed information.

How do telemedicine appointments work?

Telemedicine appointments allow you to consult with our healthcare providers remotely via secure video conferencing. Here's how it works:

  1. Schedule a telemedicine appointment through our patient portal, mobile app, or by calling our office
  2. You'll receive a confirmation email with a link to join the video call
  3. At your appointment time, click the link to join the video call from your computer, tablet, or smartphone
  4. The provider will join the call to conduct your consultation
  5. If needed, the provider can send prescriptions to your pharmacy electronically

You'll need a reliable internet connection and a device with a camera and microphone for the best experience.

Do you offer COVID-19 testing?

Yes, we offer COVID-19 testing services, including:

  • PCR tests
  • Rapid antigen tests
  • Antibody tests

Testing is available by appointment. Please call our dedicated COVID-19 hotline at (212) 555-0123 ext. 3 to schedule a test or for more information about our testing protocols.

What COVID-19 safety measures do you have in place?

We have implemented comprehensive safety measures to protect our patients and staff, including:

  • Enhanced cleaning and disinfection protocols
  • Staff wearing appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE)
  • Social distancing measures in waiting areas
  • Pre-appointment screening for COVID-19 symptoms
  • Separate areas for patients with respiratory symptoms
  • Telemedicine options to reduce in-person visits when appropriate

We continue to follow CDC and local health department guidelines to ensure the safety of everyone at our facility.
